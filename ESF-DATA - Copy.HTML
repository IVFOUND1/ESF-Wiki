Prologue — The Foundation

Beneath the capital’s humming transit lines and antiseptic museums, there existed a place no map would acknowledge and no archive would admit: the Entity Secure Foundation (ESF).

On paper, it was a medical research initiative—buried beneath shell corporations, humanitarian grants, and disaster-response rhetoric. In practice, it was a cathedral of engineered suffering.

People vanished quietly. Prisoners with no next of kin. Refugees without documentation. Children whose names were misspelled just enough to be erased. Inside the Foundation, names were a liability. Subjects were given numbers, engraved into steel doors and embedded into flesh.

The experiments were brutally simple in theory. Pain was the catalyst. Bones shattered, muscles dissolved, minds submerged in terror. Most subjects begged for death. Some received it. Others survived. Almost all experiments failed. Almost.

The Vitalis Compound

A chemical synthesis derived from adrenaline analogs, neuro-protective polymers, and a molecular stabilizer prevented systemic organ collapse. Named Vitalis, it allowed survival through the Foundation’s most extreme experiments, creating the first True Entities.
